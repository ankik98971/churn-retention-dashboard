# Business Case: Reducing Customer Churn

**To:** VP of Customer Success  
**From:** Business Analyst — Customer Retention Initiative  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Re:** Revenue impact of churn and the case for proactive retention

---

## Why Churn Matters

Customer churn is not just a retention metric — it is a direct drag on revenue and lifetime value. Every customer who leaves takes with them not only their future monthly payments, but also the acquisition cost already sunk to win them.

In our current base, **27.8% of customers churn annually**. The majority of these are on month-to-month contracts, which offer flexibility to the customer but create volatility for the business.

## Revenue at Risk

To quantify the problem in financial terms, we calculate **Revenue at Risk**:

> **Revenue at Risk** = (Average Monthly Charge of Churned Customers) × 12 months × (Number of Churned Customers)

This figure answers the question: *"If we do nothing, how much annual recurring revenue are we walking away from?"*

For the current filtered segment, this estimate is displayed live on the dashboard and updates as stakeholders apply filters (e.g., "What is the revenue at risk just among month-to-month customers with tenure under 12 months?").

## The Opportunity

Analysis shows that **contract type is the single strongest predictor of churn**:
- Month-to-month: ~40% annual churn
- One-year: ~11% annual churn  
- Two-year: ~3% annual churn

This means the business has a lever: **converting month-to-month customers to annual contracts** is not merely a pricing change — it is a retention strategy with measurable ROI.

## Recommendation

Approve a pilot retention program targeting the top two highest-ROI actions (contract-upgrade incentives and proactive outreach for high-charge, low-tenure customers). The dashboard provides live estimates of cost, target population, and revenue saved for each action.
